Monterrey, Nuevo León

European Robotics Firm Establishes Mexican Subsidiary

A mid-sized German industrial robotics manufacturer sought to capture growing automotive demand in northern Mexico but lacked local legal, operational, and financial infrastructure to do so independently.

The Challenge

Complex IMMEX certification process, no established Mexican entity, no distribution relationships with local Tier 1 automotive suppliers, and limited capital for initial facility investment.

The Settlinks Solution
  • ✓ Acted as legal representative and co-shareholder during entity setup
  • ✓ Secured IMMEX certification and industrial park space in Monterrey
  • ✓ Provided bridge capital for facility build-out and initial inventory
  • ✓ Established distribution agreements with 3 automotive Tier 1 suppliers
Querétaro & Monterrey

Asian IoT Provider Penetrates Mexico's Automotive Market

A South Korean IoT and predictive maintenance platform company had a proven product but no pathway to Mexico's automotive OEM procurement processes, no local legal presence, and needed capital to fund a 12-month pilot program.

The Challenge

Long OEM procurement cycles, language and regulatory barriers, need for local technical support staff, and a capital gap for the pilot deployment phase before recurring revenues began.

The Settlinks Solution
  • ✓ Provided venture capital funding for 12-month pilot program
  • ✓ Served as legal representative for contract negotiations with OEMs
  • ✓ Recruited bilingual technical support team in Querétaro
  • ✓ Facilitated integration with 2 major automotive Tier 1 suppliers

US Cobot Manufacturer Scales to 200% Production Growth

A Texas-based collaborative robot manufacturer with strong North American sales sought to establish Mexican production capacity to serve USMCA markets at a lower cost basis while reducing cross-border logistics complexity.

The Challenge

Identifying optimal production location, securing skilled automation technicians and engineers locally, navigating IMMEX for component imports, and scaling from zero to full production volume within 12 months.

The Settlinks Solution
  • ✓ Conducted comparative site analysis across 6 Mexican industrial parks
  • ✓ Recruited 45 engineers and technicians with cobot assembly experience
  • ✓ Managed full IMMEX certification and compliance program
  • ✓ Designed and commissioned two production facilities within 8 months
