European Robotics Firm Enters Monterrey

Collaborative robot manufacturer from Stuttgart, Germany. Product: 6-axis cobots for automotive body welding.

This Stuttgart-based manufacturer had tried to enter Mexico independently for two years without success — blocked by IMMEX complexity and lack of local legal representation. Settlinks entered as a 20% equity stakeholder in a new Mexican subsidiary. We handled the full IMMEX certification, secured a facility in a Monterrey industrial park, and recruited a team of 14 certified cobot technicians from Tecnológico de Monterrey. Within 4.5 months, the facility was operational. The client secured three automotive OEM customers within six months of launch and has since expanded headcount to 38 employees.

With zero contacts in Mexico and limited Spanish-language capability, this Osaka-based IoT company needed more than a consultant — they needed a full operational partner. Settlinks deployed $1.8M in venture capital across two tranches (market entry and first customer milestone) and took a 25% stake in the Mexican entity. Our team handled legal representation, connected the client with Tier-1 automotive suppliers in Querétaro and San Luis Potosí, and managed the cultural and language bridge for negotiations. The client reached first revenue in six months and now has predictive maintenance sensors deployed across 12 automotive plants.

US Cobot Manufacturer Scales Production 200%

Texas-based collaborative robot manufacturer. Product: Mobile autonomous robots (AMRs) for warehouse and factory logistics.

Legal Representation · Operations
200%
Production Increase

This Texas-based manufacturer was already selling into Mexico but struggling with import duties and tariff exposure on US-assembled units. Settlinks restructured their Mexico approach: we established a IMMEX-compliant final assembly operation in Saltillo that allowed them to import components duty-free, assemble locally, and sell to Mexican customers as a domestic product. Settlinks acted as legal representative throughout the IMMEX process and managed the full talent recruitment program — hiring and training 52 operators, technicians, and quality engineers. Production capacity increased 200% within twelve months.

Korean PLC Maker Builds Distribution Network

Seoul-based programmable logic controller manufacturer. Product: Mid-range PLCs and HMI panels targeting SME manufacturers.

Distribution Development · Legal
18
Distributors
8 states

Rather than establishing their own facility, this Korean PLC manufacturer wanted to build a robust distributor-led sales network across Mexico first. Settlinks identified, vetted, and contracted 18 regional distributors across 8 Mexican states — from Monterrey to Guadalajara to the Bajío region. We drafted all channel agreements to protect the client's pricing and IP, trained distributor sales staff on PLC programming fundamentals, and established a support line managed from our Monterrey office. Year-one revenue exceeded $2.4M — ahead of projections — with the distribution network now forming the foundation for a future direct-presence facility.
